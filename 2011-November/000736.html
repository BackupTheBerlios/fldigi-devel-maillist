from the same FCC document:
"Accordingly, we authorize control operators to transmit the following
additional emission types and designators in the 60 meter band: CW
emissions, limited to emission 150HA1A (i.e., Morse code telegraphy);
data
emissions, limited to emission 2K80J3E (exemplified by PACTOR-III);
and RTTY emissions, limited to
emission 60H0J2D (exemplified by PSK31)."

notwithstanding the apparent typographical errors in the designators
used in quote above:

note that they are regulated based on emission designator, not any
particular digital "mode".  also within the document is the
acknowledgement that 2K80J2D and 60H0J2B emissions are generated by a
single-sideband transmitter whose -carrier- frequency is set 1.5kHz
below the target center frequency in USB mode with audio being fed
into the transmitter.

the R&O refers only to the carrier frequency of the transmitter and
the emission designation.

by the same R&O, i am not obligated to use any particular audio
frequency except those that keep my emissions within the channel's
designated 2.8kHz bandwidth because the R&O states my emission must be
within the designated bandwidth and my carrier frequency must be set a
certain way but does not prescribe an audio frequency.

it is just as plain to me when the FCC refers to a carrier frequency,
they mean the VFO frequency and they say nothing at all about which
audio frequency i use to modulate the transmitter to create the
emission.

i certainly hope the ARRL will include John Johnston W3BE in the
drafting of the guidelines you mention, since he has a unique and
clear way of explaining his understanding of the rules and regs.
