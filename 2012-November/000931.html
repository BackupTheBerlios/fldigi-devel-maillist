Hi Makoto-

Thanks for reporting this issue.

> bdialogs/fl_digi.cxx: In function 'int get_tx_char()':
> dialogs/fl_digi.cxx:6320:44: error: 'fl_utf8len1' was not declared in this scope
> dialogs/fl_digi.cxx:6378:45: error: 'fl_utf8encode' was not declared in this scope

Dave, the problem is that the new UTF8 code in fldigi 3.21.59 won't
build with fltk1.1 (this also breaks on the older Ubuntu platforms).
Fltk1.3 builds are, of course, fine.

Makoto, for your reference, the fl_utf8.h file is provided by fltk1.3
(e.g. /usr/include/FL/fl_utf8.h on Debian/Ubuntu systems).  If you have
the option of configuring your build to use fltk1.3 instead of fltk1.1,
you'll be able to proceed.

Dave, in my opinion its still "too soon" to deprecate fltk1.1 support
(since I bet that's your first thought here! ;-)
