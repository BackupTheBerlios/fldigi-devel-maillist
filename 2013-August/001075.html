I'd like to see a publicly-readable specification of how to encode and
decode this mode.  I've worked professionally with ISO standards and with
getting them through standards bodies that have different publication
requirements, and I think we can do the right thing with regard to FCC
rules and ham spirit without violating any ISO terms of use.

Briefly, ISO standards have to be purchased; that's how ISO supports
itself. However, other groups often produce "profiles" of ISO standards
and publish them freely.  For example, ISO-8601 for time format was
profiled by W3C (an industry consortium) at
http://www.w3.org/TR/NOTE-datetime and later included in recommendation
fully defined at http://www.w3.org/TR/xmlschema-2/

When we put Olivia into gMFSK, I sent a brief description and samples of
the modulation to the ARRL.  Later the ARRL updated the technical
description: http://www.arrl.org/olivia  It's possible Steve Ford and
Pawel Jalocha did this, or it might have been Dave and Steve when Dave put
the mode into fldigi; I wasn't involved at that point.

> I believe I found the DSC specs you requested by searching the

> The DSC is a synchronous system using characters composed from a ten-bit
> error-detecting code. The bits are encoded using frequency shift
> keyingat 100 Baud. For High Frequency and Medium Frequency, two tones
> 170 Hz apart on either side of the allocated frequency are used.  For
> VHF, the two frequencies used are 1300 and 2100 Hz. Each character is
> transmitted twice with a time delay. The detailed specification for DSC
> is published in the 56-page International Telecommunications Union
> <http://www.itu.int/en/Pages/default.aspx>Recommendation ITU-R M.493-12,
