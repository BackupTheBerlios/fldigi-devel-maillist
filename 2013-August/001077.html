> Dear Dave,
>
> I believe I found the DSC specs you requested by searching the
> International Telecommunications Union (ITU) website.
>
> The DSC is a synchronous system using characters composed from a ten-bit
> error-detecting code. The bits are encoded using frequency shift keying at
> 100 Baud.  For High Frequency and Medium Frequency, two tones 170 Hz
> apart on either side of the allocated frequency are used.  For VHF, the two
> frequencies used are 1300 and 2100 Hz. Each character is transmitted twice
> with a time delay.  The detailed specification for DSC is published in the
> 56-page International Telecommunications Union<http://www.itu.int/en/Pages/default.aspx>Recommendation
> ITU-R M.493-12, which I have attached for your review.
>
> Will this work for you and your open-source programming team?

> The document that Jason sent you is the proper one for the details of the
> HF and VHF DSC protocol. Note however, that there is a later version
> (493-13) which contains some changes. That is not available to the public
> on the ITU website, but needs to be purchased.
>
> One other caution I would add, is that I believe that there is no need for
> a transmit capability, only receive. In fact, I think that transmit would
> be somewhat risky since it could be used to "spoof" transmissions which
> might potentially be a security risk.  Also, transmit requires the issuance
> of an MMSI number so that the source of any transmissions can be traced.
