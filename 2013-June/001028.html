>From an outsider on the development process and one that knew nothing about
this until Remi's recent email, I think one of the questions that needs to
be answered on this is "what is lost if we lose rig support via hamlib?"

Looking at it in detail, comparing hamlib's AMATEUR RADIO support to Flrig
and RigCAT's support for many rigs (one could argue that it is the MAJORITY
of ham rigs in use), I don't see the loss of hamlib support being a major
deal...except perhaps on Linux where I'd bet that a large percentage still
use it out of a sense of tradition or to support other software.

Ultimately, if hamlib can't easily be made to work with Fldigi and the
hamlib community is not willing to make changes to make building easier,
then dropping support for hamlib seems to be the only course of action;
anything else requires too much effort that could be better focused on
Fldigi development.
