Your characterization of autoconf/automake seems reasonably fair.  But
lately I have not been running into needing bleeding edge versions.  The
other side of the coin is that many people who complain about it then
propose and implement alternative mechanisms, and those almost always
fail to do all the things auto* did, like "make distcheck" (which
includes an objdir build of the created distfile) and cross compiling.

If you're willing to use cygwin, you can probably build all the prereqs
under cygwin on windows.  fldiig is already in pkgsrc.  But I haven't
tried it (or anything else, really) on Windows.
