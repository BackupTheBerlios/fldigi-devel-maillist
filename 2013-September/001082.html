This has been seen in 3.21.73 onwards, but not in 3.71.72 and earlier:

Occasionally (fewer than 10% of transmissions) the Olivia spectrum is
inverted. Easily detected audibly as the usual "dong-ding" preamble is
transmitted as a "ding-dong". This is followed by garbage decode at the
receive end. Simple spectral inversion is confirmed by the receiving
station clicking the "Rv" button and getting correct decode.

The signal was Olivia 16/500 with TXID enabled. I didn't try it with other
modes or without TXID. 20 transmit/receive cycles was always enough to
provoke it with 3.21.73 and 3.21.75, but 100 Tx/Rx cycles didn't cause it
in 3.21.72.

OS: Debian Wheezy i686

Hardware: EeePC 901 netbook

Compiled from source. Output from ./configure attached

Several different sound devices were tried (onboard, Signalink USB and
generic USB) without change.
